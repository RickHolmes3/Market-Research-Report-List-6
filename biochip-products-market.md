<p><p>The biochip products market is segmented into active and passive chips, each serving distinct purposes. Active chips are equipped with integrated circuits and functionalities that enable them to perform tasks like data processing and real-time analysis. Conversely, passive chips lack active components and primarily serve as sensors or substrates for biochemical interactions, relying on external systems for data interpretation. Both types play crucial roles in fields such as diagnostics, drug development, and personalized medicine, driving advancements in biotechnology.</p></p>

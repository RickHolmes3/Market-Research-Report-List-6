<p><p>The low-alcohol beer market comprises two primary types: limit fermentation and dealcoholization methods. Limit fermentation involves controlling the fermentation process to produce beer with lower alcohol content while retaining flavor and aroma. In contrast, dealcoholization methods remove alcohol from fully fermented beer, often using techniques like vacuum distillation or reverse osmosis. Both approaches cater to health-conscious consumers seeking enjoyable beer options with reduced alcohol levels, driving innovation and variety within this expanding market segment.</p></p>

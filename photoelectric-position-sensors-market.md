<p><p>Photoelectric position sensors are categorized into three main types: retro-reflective sensors, diffuse-reflective sensors, and others. Retro-reflective sensors use a light source and a reflector to detect objects, providing long-range sensing capabilities. Diffuse-reflective sensors, on the other hand, detect objects by bouncing light off the target, suitable for shorter distances. Other market types include through-beam sensors, which require a direct path between the emitter and receiver, and specialized sensors for unique applications, broadening the functionality across various industries.</p></p>

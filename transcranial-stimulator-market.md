<p><p>The transcranial stimulator market primarily includes two types: transcranial electrical stimulators and transcranial magnetic stimulators. Transcranial electrical stimulators use weak electrical currents to modulate neuronal activity, often employed in treatments for depression and anxiety. In contrast, transcranial magnetic stimulators utilize magnetic fields to induce electrical currents in specific brain regions, commonly used for neurological disorders like stroke and migraines. Both types are pivotal in non-invasive brain intervention, offering therapeutic potential with growing clinical applications.</p></p>

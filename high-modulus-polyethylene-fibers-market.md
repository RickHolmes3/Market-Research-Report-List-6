<p><p>High-modulus polyethylene (HMPE) fibers are categorized based on their melting points into two types: those with a melting point below 130°C and those above 130°C. Fibers below 130°C typically exhibit flexibility and are used in applications requiring lightweight and soft characteristics, such as textile manufacturing. In contrast, fibers with a melting point above 130°C offer superior thermal stability and strength, making them suitable for demanding applications in industries like aerospace and military textiles, where durability is essential.</p></p>

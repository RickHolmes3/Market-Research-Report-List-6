<p><p>Orally Disintegrating Films (ODFs) are thin films that dissolve quickly in the mouth without the need for water, making them convenient for patients who have difficulty swallowing pills. There are two main types of ODFs: Orodispersible Films, which disintegrate in the oral cavity, and Oromucosal Films, which disintegrate in the buccal or sublingual mucosa. These films offer a convenient and patient-friendly alternative to traditional oral dosage forms, making them increasingly popular in the market.</p></p>

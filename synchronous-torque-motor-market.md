<p><p>The Synchronous Torque Motor market is divided into AC and DC types, each serving distinct applications. AC synchronous motors operate with alternating current, providing smooth and efficient torque, ideal for high-speed operations in industrial settings. They typically require a rotor's magnetic field synchronized with the stator's frequency. In contrast, DC synchronous motors utilize direct current, offering high starting torque and precise speed control, making them suitable for applications needing reliable performance, such as robotics and electric vehicles.</p></p>

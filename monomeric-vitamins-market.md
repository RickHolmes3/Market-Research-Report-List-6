<p><p>Monomeric vitamins are classified into two main categories: fat-soluble vitamins and water-soluble vitamins. Fat-soluble vitamins include vitamins A, D, E, and K, which are stored in the body's fatty tissues and liver. On the other hand, water-soluble vitamins, such as vitamin C and the B-complex vitamins, are not stored in the body and must be consumed daily. Each type of vitamin plays a crucial role in the body's overall functioning and maintaining good health.</p></p>

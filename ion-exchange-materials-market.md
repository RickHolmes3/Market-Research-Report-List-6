<p><p>Ion exchange materials are widely categorized into different types depending on their chemical composition and structure. Synthetic zeolites are crystalline aluminosilicates with a porous structure, allowing for efficient ion exchange. Polybasic acid salts are compounds containing multiple acidic functional groups for ion exchange. Hydrous oxides are hydrated metal oxides that can adsorb and exchange ions. Metal ferrocyanides are coordination compounds containing cyanide groups for ion exchange. Insoluble markets are ion exchange materials that do not dissolve in water, making them suitable for long-term use.</p></p>

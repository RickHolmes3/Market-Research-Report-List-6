<p><p>Time of Flight (ToF) cameras are used to measure the distance between the camera and objects in the scene by calculating the time it takes for light to travel from the camera to the object and back. There are two main types of ToF cameras in the market: CMOS Time-of-flight cameras and CCD Time-of-flight cameras. CMOS cameras are known for their low power consumption and compact size, while CCD cameras offer higher image quality and better sensitivity in low light conditions.</p></p>

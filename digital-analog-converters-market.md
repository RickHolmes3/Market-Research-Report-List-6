<p><p>Digital-analog converters (DACs) are electronic devices that convert digital signals into analog signals. There are several types of DACs in the market, including R-2R DAC, Thermometer-Coded DAC, Pulse-width Modulator DAC, Oversampling DAC, and Binary-weighted DAC. R-2R DAC uses a ladder network of resistors, Thermometer-Coded DAC assigns codes to temperature levels, Pulse-width Modulator DAC converts digital signals into pulse-width modulation, Oversampling DAC samples input signals at high frequencies, and Binary-weighted DAC uses a network of resistors with different binary weights to convert digital signals into analog signals.</p></p>

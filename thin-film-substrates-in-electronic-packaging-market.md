<p><p>Thin film substrates in electronic packaging refer to a type of material used as a base for mounting electronic components. There are two main types: rigid thin-film substrates and flexible thin-film substrates. Rigid substrates are solid and inflexible, providing stability and durability for components. Flexible substrates, on the other hand, are bendable and allow for more versatile applications in electronics. Both types play a crucial role in electronic packaging by providing a solid foundation for components while also allowing for various designs and form factors.</p></p>

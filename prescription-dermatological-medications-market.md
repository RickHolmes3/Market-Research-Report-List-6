<p><p>Prescription dermatological medications can be categorized into two main types: oral and topical. Oral medications are ingested and work internally to treat various skin conditions, while topical medications are applied directly to the skin surface for localized treatment. The oral market typically includes systemic medications that target underlying causes of skin issues, such as antibiotics or oral retinoids. In contrast, the topical market consists of creams, ointments, and gels that are applied externally to the affected area to provide targeted relief and treatment.</p></p>

<p><p>Non-nutritive sweeteners are substances used to sweeten food and beverages without providing significant calories or nutrients. Additionally, the shift towards healthier lifestyles has prompted manufacturers to innovate and develop new non-nutritive sweeteners that cater to evolving dietary preferences, including plant-based options.</p><p>Current trends indicate a growing preference for natural sweeteners, such as stevia and monk fruit, as consumers seek cleaner label products. The rise of e-commerce and health-focused food brands is further bolstering market demand, making non-nutritive sweeteners an integral part of the food and beverage industry. Overall, the market is adapting to meet the changing tastes and health concerns of consumers globally.</p></p>

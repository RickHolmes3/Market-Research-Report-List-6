<p><p>Coatings for medical applications come in various types to cater to different needs. Hydrophilic coatings improve surface wetting and reduce friction, making them ideal for catheters and guidewires. Antimicrobial coatings inhibit the growth of bacteria, reducing the risk of infections. Drug-eluting coatings release medication slowly over time, providing localized treatment. Anti-thrombogenic coatings prevent blood clot formation on medical devices. Other types of coatings include anti-fouling, radiopaque, and lubricious coatings, each serving a specific purpose in improving the efficacy and safety of medical devices.</p></p>
